## **Task 0 — Project Objectives**

### **0.1 Purpose (Task 0)**  
The purpose of the *Asalah* project is to provide a culturally aligned, gamified financial‑literacy platform that helps Saudi families teach children responsibility, values, and basic money management. The MVP aims to address the growing challenge of impulsive spending and limited financial awareness among children by offering an engaging, value‑based learning experience.

### **0.2 Objectives (Task 0)**  
- Enhance children’s understanding of earning, saving, and responsible spending through a non‑monetary points system (Noor Points).  
- Support parents in teaching cultural, behavioral, and value‑based habits through structured tasks.  
- Deliver a gamified experience that motivates children to stay engaged and track their progress.

## **Task 2 — Project Scope Definition**

### **2.1 Scope Overview (Task 2)**  
The MVP focuses on delivering the essential features needed to demonstrate the core concept of Asalah: teaching children values and financial awareness through tasks and a points‑based reward system. The scope excludes any advanced or external integrations to keep development realistic and achievable.

---

### **2.2 In‑Scope Items (Task 2)**  
- Parent interface for creating and managing tasks.  
- Child interface for viewing and completing tasks.  
- Noor Points system (virtual, non‑monetary).  
- Tasks related to values, responsibility, culture, and positive habits.  
- Support for children aged **6–16**.  
- Basic reward redemption system.  
- Simple child progress dashboard.

---

### **2.3 Out‑of‑Scope Items (Task 2)**  
- Features for users older than **16 years**.  
- Real‑money transactions or financial transfers.  
- Integration with banks or digital wallets.  
- School partnerships or institutional programs.  
- Advanced features (AI suggestions, analytics, family challenges).  
- Public social or community features.

## **Task 3 — Risk Analysis & Mitigation Plans**

### **3.1 Risk Overview (Task 3)**  
This section identifies potential challenges that may arise during development and outlines strategies to reduce their impact.

---

### **3.2 Risks & Mitigation (Task 3)**  

| Risk | Description | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Lack of experience with some tools | Team members may face difficulty with new technologies. | Allocate learning time, use tutorials, and divide tasks based on strengths. |
| Delays due to time pressure | Academic workload may affect task completion. | Create a clear timeline, assign weekly tasks, and track progress in meetings. |
| Uneven commitment levels | Variation in team engagement may slow progress. | Define responsibilities clearly and follow up regularly. |
| Feature complexity | Some features may exceed MVP limits. | Stick strictly to MVP scope and postpone advanced features. |
| Frontend‑Backend integration issues | Errors may occur during API connection. | Test integration early and document APIs clearly. |
| Unclear requirements | Some details may become ambiguous during development. | Discuss in meetings and update documentation as needed. |
