# Portfolio Project — Stage 1 Report  
## Team Formation and Idea Development

## Project Name  
**Asalah — A Gamified Value-Based Financial Literacy App**

---

## Concept Overview

**Asalah**, meaning **authenticity and rootedness** in Arabic, is a gamified financial literacy and rewards platform designed specifically for Saudi families. The app reflects the goal of connecting modern financial literacy with Saudi family values, traditions, and cultural identity.

Asalah bridges the gap between traditional values and modern financial education by allowing parents to reward children for activities rooted in Saudi culture, faith-based values, family traditions, responsibility, and positive financial habits.

---

This report presents the outcomes of Stage 1 of the Portfolio Project: Team Formation and Idea Development. The idea combines financial education, family values, cultural identity, and gamification to help children develop responsible financial habits in an engaging and meaningful way.

### 7. Brainstorming Techniques Used

The team organized brainstorming sessions to generate, discuss, and refine ideas. Three structured techniques were used.

#### 7.1 Mind Mapping

The team used mind mapping to visually connect related ideas and explore different problem areas. This helped the team identify common themes such as family, education, financial responsibility, digital habits, culture, community, and accessibility.

#### 7.2 SCAMPER Framework

The team applied the SCAMPER framework to improve and transform existing ideas.

The team considered how to:

- **Substitute** traditional reward systems with gamified points
- **Combine** financial literacy with cultural and family values
- **Adapt** gamification methods for educational purposes
- **Modify** existing family task systems into a structured digital platform
- **Put to another use** reward points as a tool for teaching saving and responsibility
- **Eliminate** unnecessary complexity such as real banking integration in the MVP
- **Reverse** the usual spending-first mindset by encouraging earning, saving, and value-based rewards

#### 7.3 “How Might We” Questions

The team used open-ended “How Might We” questions to frame problems and opportunities.

Examples included:

- How might we help children understand the value of money in an engaging way?
- How might we support parents in teaching financial responsibility at home?
- How might we reduce impulsive spending habits among children?
- How might we connect modern technology with Saudi family values?
- How might we make financial literacy fun and age-appropriate for children?

## Task 3: Decision and Refinement

### 13. Selected Final MVP Idea

After evaluating all proposed ideas, the team selected **Asalah** as the final MVP concept.

The decision was made collectively based on the evaluation results, team discussion, feasibility, user value, and technical suitability. Asalah received the highest score and showed the strongest alignment with the project goals.

### 14. Refined MVP Concept

**Asalah** means **authenticity, originality, and rootedness** in Arabic. The name reflects the app’s purpose: connecting modern financial literacy with Saudi family values, culture, and positive habits.

Asalah is a gamified value-based financial literacy platform designed for Saudi families. It helps parents teach children financial responsibility by assigning meaningful tasks and rewarding completed actions with **Noor Points**.

The platform does not involve real money transfers or banking integration in the MVP. Instead, it uses a points-based reward system to teach children concepts such as earning, saving, budgeting, giving, and responsible spending.

### 15. Problem Statement

Saudi families are increasingly facing challenges related to children’s impulsive spending, digital consumption, and limited awareness of financial responsibility. Many children are exposed to digital games, online purchases, and consumer-driven content at a young age.

Existing financial education tools often lack cultural relevance, family involvement, and child-friendly engagement. As a result, parents may struggle to teach children financial values in a consistent, interactive, and meaningful way.

### 16. Target Audience

#### Primary Users

Saudi parents aged approximately **25–45** who want to teach their children responsible spending habits, saving, family values, and positive behavior.

#### Secondary Users

Children aged approximately **6–15** who are influenced by digital consumption, gaming, and reward-based systems.

### 17. Core Idea

The core idea of Asalah is to allow parents to reward children for completing tasks related to:

- Family participation
- Responsibility
- Cultural values
- Faith-based and value-based habits
- Saving and budgeting
- Reducing screen time
- Respectful behavior
- Helping at home

Children earn **Noor Points**, which they can later use to request rewards or wishes approved by their parents.

### 18. MVP Features

#### Parent Interface

Parents can:

- Create an account
- Add child profiles
- Create and assign tasks
- Categorize tasks by type
- Approve or reject completed tasks
- Assign Noor Points
- Create rewards or wishes
- View child progress

#### Child Interface

Children can:

- View assigned tasks
- Mark tasks as completed
- Track earned Noor Points
- View available rewards
- Request to redeem rewards
- See progress in a child-friendly dashboard

#### Task Categories

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Tradition and Culture | Participating in family gatherings, serving guests, respecting elders |
| Faith-Based and Value-Based Habits | Prayer consistency, memorizing short Surahs, charity habits |
| Chores and Responsibility | Cleaning a room, helping with household tasks, organizing school items |
| Financial Values | Saving points, budgeting rewards, choosing between spending and saving |
| Screen-Time Balance | Disconnecting from screens during family time |

### 19. Reward Mechanics

The reward system is based on **Noor Points**.

Noor Points are non-monetary points that children earn after completing parent-approved tasks. Children can collect points and use them to request rewards, wishes, or privileges set by their parents.

Examples of rewards:

- Choosing a family activity
- Getting extra playtime
- Buying a small item
- Saving points for a larger wish
- Choosing a weekend activity
- Donating points toward charity with parental guidance

### 20. Expected Outcomes

The expected outcomes of Asalah include:

- Improving children’s understanding of earning, saving, and spending
- Encouraging responsible behavior through positive reinforcement
- Helping parents teach financial values in a structured way
- Reducing impulsive reward-seeking behavior
- Strengthening the connection between family values and financial habits
- Providing a culturally relevant financial literacy experience for Saudi families

### 21. Future Enhancements

The following features may be considered in future versions after the MVP:

- Achievement badges
- Streaks and levels
- AI-based task suggestions
- Family challenges
- School or educational partnerships
- Bank or savings account integration
- Parent focus groups
- Pilot launch with 10 Saudi families

## 27. Final MVP Summary

**Asalah** is a gamified value-based financial literacy platform designed for Saudi families. It helps parents teach children responsible financial behavior through culturally relevant tasks, positive habits, and a points-based reward system.

Parents can create tasks, assign Noor Points, approve child progress, and define rewards. Children can view tasks, complete them, earn points, and request rewards. The MVP focuses on education, motivation, and family involvement rather than real financial transactions.

The project was selected because it is culturally relevant, socially impactful, technically feasible, and scalable. It also provides a clear opportunity to combine frontend development, backend logic, database design, user experience, and testing.
